ASTEROID'S "CLOSE APPROACH" DAY

The Asteroid Apophis made an “Close Approach” today. It officially crossed paths with the Earth’s orbit at 8:59 a.m. this morning. Named after the Egyptian god Apophis the Great Serpent, the asteroid is almost as tall as the Empire State Building. NASA suggests a 2.7 percent chance of Apophis striking Earth in 2029.

FULL DAY ELLIPSE

Areas including the Republic of Congo, the Democratic Republic of Congo, Sudan, Ethiopia, Yemen, India and China all witnessed the day eclipse today. It’s the most full day eclipse ever witnessed in China in the 21st century.
